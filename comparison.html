Comparison

We set our own cielings - YMCA Nationals is too low

The argument is not that we must win the other meets, but that by setting our sights on bigger stages we'll better serve everyone. Youth development relies on the teaching and understanding of hard work, goal setting, and dealing with adversity. If our training and goals are low we can never promise to develop the athletes adequatly.

Current

Lower ceiling, compressed results

When the goal standard for a program's top group is modest, training naturally orients toward achieving that standard — and stops there. Workouts, expectations, and competitive targets all calibrate to a lower ceiling.

The result is a tight, left-leaning distribution. Most athletes cluster around a narrow performance band, top-end outliers are rare, and the program's overall talent spread remains limited. The goal dictates the training, and the training dictates the outcome.

Goal

Higher ceiling, broader talent spread

When the top group trains toward an elite national standard, the entire program is pulled upward. Training loads, time standards, race strategy, and daily expectations all rise to match the ceiling — not just for the top group, but throughout the pipeline below it.

The distribution widens and shifts right. The mean rises, the spread broadens, and elite outliers appear naturally. A higher goal produces a harder training environment, which in turn produces a deeper and more capable roster across all levels.
